# Part 3 — Right-of-Way Signs (301–310)

## Category Overview

- **Hebrew:** תמרורי זכות קדימה
- **Purpose:** Establish who has right-of-way at intersections, narrow sections, and special locations
- **Shapes:** Various — inverted triangle (yield), octagon (stop), diamond (priority road), arrow (narrow section)
- **Color:** White background with red/black markings; priority road signs are yellow diamond

---

### Sign 302 — STOP

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Number** | 302 |
| **Hebrew Term** | עצור! |
| **Shape / Color** | Octagonal white sign with red border and blocking circle (minimum diameter 65 cm) |
| **Image** | `../../images/sign_302.png` |
| **Meaning** | STOP — come to a complete stop, then yield right-of-way to all traffic on the cross road (including all lanes). If placed on left side of road only: applies to left turns and left U-turns only. If placed above road: applies only to lane directly below |
| **Applicability** | At the placement location; all lanes unless otherwise restricted |
| **Variants** | — |
| **Cross-references** | 301, 303, 304, 305, 810 |
| **Paired with** | — |
| **Notes** | Minimum blocking-circle diameter: 65 cm per ordinance Section 4(d)(6). Stop line (810) is placed at the stop point |
