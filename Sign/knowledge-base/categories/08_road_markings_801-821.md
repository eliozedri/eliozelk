# Part 8: Road Surface Marking Signs (801–821)

**Hebrew:** תמרורי ניסוי על פני הדרך
**Sign range:** 801–821
**Shape/Color:** Lines, symbols, and text painted or applied on the road surface — White, Yellow, or Blue
**General rule:** Road surface markings carry the same legal force as upright signs. Where a surface marking conflicts with an upright sign, the upright sign takes precedence unless otherwise specified. Markings on the road surface are listed here as "signs on the road" (תמרורים על פני הדרך).

## Longitudinal Line Markings (801–811)

### Sign 801 — SOLID WHITE CENTRE LINE

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Number** | 801 |
| **Hebrew Term** | קו לבן רצוף — קו המפריד |
| **Category** | Road Surface Markings |
| **Shape / Color** | Continuous white line along road centre |
| **Image** | `../../images/sign_801.png` |
| **Meaning** | Separates opposing traffic flows. Do not cross or straddle this line except to avoid an obstacle, enter/exit a side road, or in an emergency. |
| **Applicability** | Applies to all vehicles on roads where this line is marked. |
| **Variants** | — |
| **Cross-references** | 802, 803 |
| **Paired with** | — |
| **Notes** | A solid white line is more restrictive than a broken line; no overtaking across solid line on the driver's side. |
