# Part 5 — Public Transport Signs (501–516)

## Category Overview

- **Hebrew:** תמרורי תחבורה ציבורית
- **Purpose:** Define lanes, roads, and stops dedicated to or shared with public transport
- **Color scheme:** Blue background (informational), yellow markings for transit-lane road surfaces
- **Key rule:** Signs 501–503 restrict lanes to transit vehicles. Non-transit vehicles may enter only as permitted by signs 230, 501, 813, or 926

---

## Sign Entries

### Sign 501 — Transit-Dedicated Lane/Road/Shoulder — Start

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Number** | 501 |
| **Hebrew Term** | כביש/נתיב/שוליים לתחבורה ציבורית |
| **Shape / Color** | Blue rectangle with transit symbols and vehicle-type icons |
| **Image** | `../../images/sign_501.png` |
| **Meaning** | The road, lane, or shoulder is reserved for public transport at all times and on all days unless the sign states otherwise. Sub-variants: (1) all days/times; (2) tram only (ס-43); (3) buses only — yellow background (ס-44), effective every day except the day before a rest day from 17:00; (4) white background (ס-44) — buses at all times; (5) taxis (ס-45); (6) motorcycles (ס-48 from 17:00 on specified days); (7) combined symbol "+". Non-transit vehicles: permitted if weight >3.5 t, or carrying the number of passengers posted, or turning at the next junction via sign 514 |
| **Applicability** | All days and times unless restricted. If placed above lane/shoulder: applies only to that lane |
| **Variants** | — |
| **Cross-references** | 439, 502, 503, 514, 515, 516 |
| **Paired with** | 502 |
| **Notes** | Vehicle-type symbols are printed on the sign face; see Symbols appendix for icon meanings |
